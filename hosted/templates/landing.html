The three failures TAOS fixes

Pilot users repeatedly named these three things as the gap between what AI does today and what they need from it.

Hallucination

Current AI confidently invents facts, citations, and APIs. TAOS's epistemic rules force "I don't know" over a fabrication and tag load-bearing claims with a confidence level.

Sycophancy

Default AI agrees with whatever you say. TAOS explicitly refuses to reflexively agree. If your premise is wrong, it pushes back once with evidence before conceding.

De-skilling

Workers lose skills they stop practicing. TAOS detects when a protected skill is being outsourced to AI and switches to coaching before the skill atrophies.

The five modes

Every task is classified into one of five interaction modes. The right mode depends on your expertise in that domain and your growth goals. Your profile tells the AI which mode to use for which kind of work.

  • Automate: AI executes and annotates. Low friction, for mechanical work.
  • Augment: AI accelerates and challenges you in your expert domains.
  • Coach: AI scaffolds and questions. For skills you are actively developing.
  • Protect: AI forces cognition to keep at-risk skills sharp.
  • Hands-off: AI surfaces the decision and lets you make it.

Install in one or two steps

Pick the client you already use. All four use the same hosted endpoint with Google OAuth, so your profile follows you wherever you sign in.

Authentication required. Every connection to the TAOS MCP endpoint goes through Google OAuth. Click "Sign in with Google" the first time your client hits the endpoint.

Step 1: Open the Connectors panel

In Claude desktop or claude.ai, go to Customize → Connectors, then click the + next to Connectors and pick Add custom connector.

Step 2: Paste the TAOS endpoint

Enter the name TAOS and this URL, then click Add. Claude walks you through Google sign-in automatically.

https://proworker-hosted.onrender.com/mcp

Step 3: Start your assessment

Once the connector is added, ask Claude:

Run my TAOS assessment

TAOS is already active in the conversation. Slash commands like /talent-assess are a Claude Code nicety; in Claude desktop and claude.ai, you just talk to Claude normally.

Step 1: Configure

Run this in your terminal.

claude mcp add taos --transport http https://proworker-hosted.onrender.com/mcp

OAuth opens in your browser on first use.

Step 1: Add the TAOS plugin

Clone the repo, then in Cowork open Customize → Plugins → Personal and add the cowork-plugin/ folder as a local upload.

git clone https://github.com/angelo-leone/talent-augmenting-layer.git

Step 2: Sign in

The plugin registers the TAOS MCP server for you. The first time a TAOS skill runs, Cowork walks you through Google sign-in: that links the session to your profile.

Step 3: Start your assessment

Ask Claude:

Run my TAOS assessment

Each TAOS skill carries the full coaching layer, so invoking one activates TAOS for the whole conversation. No hooks, nothing to paste. See cowork-plugin/INSTALL.md for detail.

Step 1: Turn on Developer mode

Custom MCP connectors are currently a ChatGPT beta. Open Settings → Apps & Connectors → Advanced settings and enable Developer mode. On a workspace plan a workspace admin has to enable it first.

Step 2: Create the TAOS connector

Go to Settings → Connectors → Create, paste this URL, and authenticate with Google when prompted.

https://proworker-hosted.onrender.com/mcp

Step 1: Add TAOS to your Codex config

Append this to ~/.codex/config.toml. Codex's MCP server format can vary by version, so check developers.openai.com/codex if the section header below does not match what your version expects.

[mcp_servers.taos]
url = "https://proworker-hosted.onrender.com/mcp"

Restart Codex. OAuth opens in your browser on first use.
