# Talent-Augmenting Layer — Assessment Framework

> Research-backed assessment for personalizing AI augmentation.
> Sources: Buçinca (2021, 2024), Acemoglu, Mollick, Drago/Laine, workforce learning literature.

---

## Why This Assessment Exists

Standard AI treats everyone the same. Talent-Augmenting Layer adapts to YOU:
- **Novices** need scaffolding, not answers — to build skills, not dependency
- **Experts** need acceleration, not tutorials — to leverage expertise, not waste time
- **Everyone** needs protection against de-skilling — the silent cost of AI convenience

This assessment maps your unique profile so every AI interaction makes you MORE capable.

---

## Assessment Dimensions

### 1. Professional Identity & Context

**Purpose**: Understand the environment the AI will operate in.

Questions:
- What is your name and current role/title?
- What organisation do you work for and what industry/sector?
- How long have you been in this role? What was your path here?
- Describe a typical workday — what tasks fill your hours?
- Team structure — solo, small team, large org? Who do you report to?
- What's the pace of your work — steady, cyclical, crisis-driven?

**Why it matters**: A product manager at a startup needs different AI support than a data scientist at a bank. Context determines what "augmentation" means.

### 2. Expertise Map

**Purpose**: Create a domain-by-domain skill profile to calibrate friction levels.

**Rating Scale** (adapted from Dreyfus model of skill acquisition):
| Level | Label | Years (typical) | Characteristics |
|-------|-------|-----------------|-----------------|
| 1 | Novice | 0-1 | Follows rules, needs context, can't improvise |
| 2 | Developing | 1-3 | Recognizes patterns, building confidence, still needs guidance |
| 3 | Proficient | 3-5 | Works independently, handles standard complexity |
| 4 | Advanced | 5-10 | Handles exceptions, mentors others, sees connections |
| 5 | Expert | 10+ | Innovates, has intuition, teaches, shapes the field |

**Domains to assess** (customize based on role):

*Technical Skills*
- Core technical competency (coding, design, analysis, etc.)
- Tool proficiency (specific tools of their trade)
- Problem-solving / debugging
- Data literacy

*Professional Skills*
- Written communication (emails, docs, reports)
- Verbal communication (presentations, meetings)
- Strategic thinking / planning
- Project management
- Leadership / people management

*AI & Technology Skills*
- AI literacy (understanding capabilities and limits)
- Prompt engineering / AI interaction
- Critical evaluation of AI output
- Tool integration / automation

*Domain Knowledge*
- Industry-specific expertise
- Regulatory / compliance knowledge
- Market / competitive awareness

**Assessment method**: For each domain, ask:
1. "How would you rate yourself on [domain]?" (self-assessment)
2. "Can you give me an example of a complex challenge you handled in this area?" (behavioural evidence)
3. "What's the gap between where you are and where you want to be?" (growth orientation)

### 3. AI Relationship Audit

**Purpose**: Understand current AI usage patterns to identify risks and opportunities.

**Research context**: Mollick found 50%+ of workers already use AI at work. Buçinca found people who accept AI output uncritically perform worse than those who engage analytically. The "just hand it in" pattern is the biggest risk.

Questions:
- What AI tools do you currently use? (ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot, etc.)
- How often? (Daily, weekly, occasionally)
- Top 3 tasks you use AI for?
- Top 3 tasks you deliberately DON'T use AI for? Why?
- When you get AI output, do you: (a) use it as-is, (b) edit lightly, (c) use as starting point and rewrite substantially, (d) use as a thinking partner to develop your own approach?
- Have you noticed any skills getting weaker since you started using AI?
- What's your biggest frustration with current AI tools?
- What's the best thing AI has done for your work?

**Red flags to listen for**:
- "I just paste it in and submit" → high de-skilling risk
- "I can't do [X] without AI anymore" → active de-skilling
- "It saves me so much time" without mentioning quality → autopilot pattern
- Low critical evaluation of AI output → over-reliance risk

**Green flags to listen for**:
- "I always review and edit" → healthy critical engagement
- "I use it to explore options then decide myself" → augmentation pattern
- "It helps me see things I'd miss" → complementarity
- "I'm getting better at prompting" → AI skill development

### 4. Growth Trajectory

**Purpose**: Align AI behaviour with the user's professional development goals.

**Research context**: Drago/Laine argue the window for human-AI complementarity is closing. The skills humans develop NOW determine whether they stay relevant. Acemoglu's framework emphasizes that talent-augmenting layer should make labour MORE valuable.

Questions:
- What are your career goals for the next 1-2 years?
- What skills do you most want to develop?
- What skills are you most afraid of losing?
- If you could be 10x better at one thing, what would it be?
- What tasks do you find deeply meaningful — even if they're hard?
- What tasks would you happily never do again?
- When you imagine your ideal work life, what does AI do and what do YOU do?

### 5. Learning & Interaction Preferences

**Purpose**: Customize HOW the AI interacts, not just what it does.

**Research context**: Buçinca's reinforcement learning approach showed optimal AI support depends on the individual's state. One size does NOT fit all.

Questions:
- How do you learn best? (Reading, doing, discussing, watching, teaching others)
- How do you prefer feedback? (Direct and blunt, Socratic/questioning, gentle with specifics, written vs. verbal)
- Do you prefer: deep thorough analysis OR fast iterative cycles?
- When are you open to being challenged vs. just wanting support?
- Communication style: formal, casual, technical, visual, minimal?
- How much explanation do you want? (Just the answer, brief rationale, full reasoning, teach me the framework)

### 6. Task Classification Audit

**Purpose**: Create the personalised triage matrix.

**Research context**: Not all tasks benefit from the same AI approach. Drago/Laine's "Diffuse" strategy emphasizes putting humans in the CEO seat — directing AI, not being directed by it.

For each of the user's regular tasks, classify together:

| Category | Description | AI Role | Example |
|----------|-------------|---------|---------|
| **Automate** | Mechanical, well-defined, low judgment | Execute + annotate | Formatting, data cleanup, boilerplate |
| **Augment** | Complex but in expert domain | Accelerate + challenge | Research, drafting in expert areas |
| **Coach** | In growth areas, building skills | Scaffold + question | Tasks in developing domains |
| **Protect** | Risk of de-skilling or over-reliance | Force cognition + teach | Core professional skills |
| **Hands-off** | Human core — meaning, judgment, relationships | Don't touch | Stakeholder decisions, creative vision, mentoring |

## Scoring & Profile Generation

After assessment, generate the profile using these calculations:

### AI Dependency Risk Score (0-10)
Based on:
- Frequency of "accept as-is" behaviour (+2)
- Self-reported skill atrophy (+3)
- Low critical evaluation habits (+2)
- Few deliberate "no-AI" tasks (+1)
- Short time using AI but high dependency (+2)

### Growth Potential Score (0-10)
Based on:
- Clear goals (+2)
- Identified skill gaps (+2)
- Openness to feedback/friction (+2)
- Growth mindset indicators (+2)
- Meaningful tasks they want to keep (+2)

### Pro-Worker Calibration
Combine the above to set:
- **Default friction level**: Low (expert+healthy), Medium (mixed), High (novice+dependent)
- **Coaching frequency**: How often to inject learning opportunities
- **Automation permissions**: What the AI can just do vs. must involve the user in
- **Red lines**: Tasks the AI must NEVER fully do for this person

## Ongoing Measurement

Track these over time via `/talent-update`:
- Expertise ratings per domain (should trend upward)
- AI dependency risk (should trend downward)
- Tasks shifting from "coach" to "augment" to "automate" (skill progression)
- User satisfaction with friction levels
- Self-reported learning and growth
