# Publication Venue Analysis: Pro-Worker AI Benchmark Paper

## Recommended Strategy

The **dual-track approach** maximizes both speed-to-visibility and publication quality:
1. Submit a **workshop paper** for early feedback and community building
2. Submit the **full paper** to a top venue

---

## Top 10 Venues (Ranked by Fit)

### 1. ACM FAccT 2027 — Conference on Fairness, Accountability, and Transparency
- **Why best fit:** FAccT explicitly welcomes cross-disciplinary work at AI evaluation × labor economics × responsible AI. The benchmark's normative claim (AI should augment workers) and grounding in Acemoglu's economics + Buçinca's HCI research is perfect for this venue.
- **Format:** Up to 14 pages excluding references. Archival or non-archival option.
- **Deadline:** ~January 2027
- **Acceptance rate:** 24–27%
- **Prestige:** Top-tier for responsible AI. Published in ACM proceedings.

### 2. ACM CHI 2027 — Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems
- **Why excellent fit:** CHI is the premier HCI conference. Buçinca's cognitive forcing research was published at CHI, establishing precedent. CHI 2026 has directly relevant workshops on "Tools for Thought" and "Human-Centered Evaluation of LMs."
- **Format:** No fixed page limit; 7,000–8,000 words typical.
- **Deadline:** ~September 2026
- **Acceptance rate:** ~25%
- **Prestige:** CORE A*. The top HCI venue worldwide.

### 3. ACM CSCW 2026/2027 — Computer-Supported Cooperative Work
- **Why excellent fit:** CSCW focuses on how technology affects collaborative work—directly relevant to the complementarity and skill preservation dimensions.
- **Format:** 5,000–12,000 words via PACM HCI journal track.
- **Deadline:** Rolling deadlines starting early 2026
- **Acceptance rate:** ~22–25%
- **Prestige:** CORE A*. Top-tier work-focused HCI venue.

### 4. NeurIPS 2026 Datasets & Benchmarks Track
- **Why strong fit:** Dedicated track for novel evaluation frameworks. PWI benchmark with 7-dimension scoring and LLM-as-judge methodology is exactly what this track solicits.
- **Format:** 9-page limit. Code/data must be publicly available.
- **Deadline:** ~May 2026
- **Acceptance rate:** ~25–26%
- **Prestige:** CORE A*. One of the top 3 ML conferences globally.

### 5. AIES 2026 — AAAI/ACM Conference on AI, Ethics, and Society
- **Why strong fit:** Explicitly designed for interdisciplinary AI-society connections. Tracks on human-centered AI, value alignment, trustworthy AI.
- **Format:** Up to 10 pages in AAAI format.
- **Deadline:** ~May 2026 (Malmö, Sweden, October 2026)
- **Acceptance rate:** 25–30%
- **Prestige:** Well-regarded interdisciplinary venue.

### 6. COLM 2026 — Conference on Language Modeling
- **Why good fit:** Newer conference dedicated to LLMs. Explicitly lists "evaluation (benchmarks, scalable oversight, evaluation protocols)" as a topic.
- **Format:** Strict 9-page limit.
- **Deadline:** March 31, 2026 ← **UPCOMING**
- **Acceptance rate:** ~25–30% (estimated)
- **Prestige:** Growing rapidly. Focused LLM venue.

### 7. ACM IUI 2027 — Intelligent User Interfaces
- **Why good fit:** Sits at the AI×HCI intersection. Cognitive forcing and complementarity are core IUI concerns. Top papers invited to ACM TiiS journal.
- **Format:** ~10,000 words max.
- **Deadline:** ~October 2026
- **Acceptance rate:** ~24%
- **Prestige:** CORE A conference.

### 8. EMNLP 2026 — Empirical Methods in NLP
- **Why good fit:** Top NLP venue valuing empirical evaluation methodology. LLM-as-judge approach fits well. Has "Findings" track as secondary landing spot.
- **Format:** Long papers up to 8 pages via ACL Rolling Review.
- **Deadline:** Rolling via ARR; commitment ~June–July 2026
- **Acceptance rate:** ~20–25%
- **Prestige:** CORE A*. Top 3 NLP venue.

### 9. ACM TOCHI Journal — Transactions on Computer-Human Interaction
- **Why good fit:** Premier HCI journal. Rolling submissions (no deadline pressure). Papers can be presented at CHI starting 2026.
- **Format:** No page limit (typically 20–40 pages published).
- **Deadline:** Rolling (submit anytime)
- **Acceptance rate:** Highly selective but no fixed rate.
- **Prestige:** Impact factor 6.6 (2024). Q1 in CS and HCI.

### 10. CHI 2026 Workshop: "Tools for Thought" (W60)
- **Why good near-term option:** Directly addresses protecting human cognition from AI erosion. Fastest path to presenting at a prestigious venue. Non-archival (doesn't preclude full publication).
- **Format:** 2–6 page extended abstract.
- **Deadline:** Check if HCI Journal special issue still accepting.
- **Prestige:** Workshop at CORE A* conference.
